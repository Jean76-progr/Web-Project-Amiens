The Rich History of Amiens

Discover the stories that shaped our beautiful city

Historical Timeline

1st Century BC

Amiens is founded by the Gauls as Samarobriva, later becoming an important Roman city.

1220

Construction of the Amiens Cathedral begins, marking the start of a new era for the city.

15th-16th Centuries

Amiens becomes a major textile center, known for its production of velour and other fabrics.

1802

The Treaty of Amiens is signed, bringing a brief peace during the Napoleonic Wars.

1918

The Battle of Amiens marks a turning point in World War I, leading to Allied victory.

1940-1944

Amiens is occupied during World War II and suffers significant damage.

Post-1945

Amiens undergoes significant reconstruction and modernization.

Historical Landmarks

Amiens Cathedral

A UNESCO World Heritage site, this Gothic masterpiece is the largest cathedral in France.

Les Hortillonnages

These ancient floating gardens have been cultivated since the Middle Ages.

Jules Verne House

The famous author's residence, now a museum dedicated to his life and works.
